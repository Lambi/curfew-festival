01 — Visual Direction

Thermal
Brutalism

Type that's too big for the frame. Thermal bloom orbs that eat into letterforms like heat signatures from a crowd. Razor-thin geometric cuts slicing through the organic. Data coordinates whispering underneath. The system borrows from Polish poster art, FORZA's sculptural boldness, and the Design & AI Symposium's generative DNA. Warm. Raw. Arresting.

Type That Bleeds
Headlines fill the frame. Get cropped. Break the edge. Big Shoulders Display at maximum weight, minimum tracking. The type is the poster.
Thermal Orbs
Soft radial circles. Screen blend mode. They overlap the type, creating depth. Like infrared shots of a crowd. Never hard-edged. Always radial-gradient to transparent.
Razor Cuts
1-3px lines at 4% opacity. Vertical and horizontal. They slash through the composition like a grid that's almost invisible. Structure through absence.
Data Ghosts
Monospaced coordinates at 12% opacity. They drift across the canvas. Residue from the generative system that mapped the festival's soul. Never readable, always felt.

06
06 — Guidelines

Rules

Always

  • Type fills the frame. If it feels too big, double it.
  • Max 2 thermal orbs per composition. Constraint = identity.
  • Screen blend mode on orbs. They must eat into the type.
  • 1–2 razor cuts per layout. 2px wide, 4% opacity.
  • Heavy film grain on everything. This isn't clean.
  • Zero border-radius. Not on buttons, not on cards, not anywhere.
  • 1px grid borders at 4% opacity. Structure through whispers.
  • Negative space is sacred. 60% empty minimum.

Never

  • White backgrounds. This lives in the dark.
  • Thermal colors on text. They only exist as orbs.
  • More than 3 elements on a social post. Poster discipline.
  • Drop shadows. Depth comes from blend modes only.
  • Decorative flourishes. Everything earns its pixel.
  • Rounded corners. Anywhere. For any reason.
  • Small type as the hero. If it's not massive, rethink.
  • Inter, Roboto, system fonts. We have our three. That's enough.
