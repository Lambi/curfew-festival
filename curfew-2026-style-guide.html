Visual Direction — 01

Signal Bloom

The 2026 visual identity is called "Signal Bloom." It draws from two sources: Curfew's warm, analog heritage (golden hour, disco balls, film grain) and the language of generative data visualization (soft organic clusters, coordinate markers, algorithmic patterns). The result feels both human and futuristic: like looking at the festival through a thermal lens.

Organic Clusters

Soft, radial gradient circles in varying sizes and opacities. They cluster and overlap like cells under a microscope or heat signatures from a crowd. Always use radial gradients that fade to transparent, never hard edges.

Data Markers

Small monospaced coordinate labels float across compositions like metadata from a generative system. They add a layer of intentional "noise" that makes every piece feel computed yet poetic. Use at 25% opacity max.

Sharp Geometry

Rectangular blocks at low opacity cut through the organic forms. They create visual tension and structure like grid lines in a data visualization. These ground the softness and prevent compositions from feeling too ethereal.

Typography Dominance

Bold Big Shoulders Display headlines break through the bloom layer. Text is never secondary to graphics. The interplay between massive display type and the organic signal field behind it is the signature look.

Guidelines — 06

Do's & Don'ts

Rules to keep the Signal Bloom identity consistent across all touchpoints.

Do

  • Use bloom circles with radial-gradient fading to transparent
  • Keep coordinate labels at very low opacity (max 25%)
  • Let Big Shoulders Display headlines cut through the bloom layer
  • Use the grain texture overlay on all digital surfaces
  • Keep bloom accents in the background; text always readable
  • Mix warm heritage colors with cool bloom accents
  • Use sharp-edged buttons to contrast the organic shapes
  • Apply mix-blend-mode: screen on bloom circles over dark backgrounds

Don't

  • Use hard-edged circles or flat color fills for bloom elements
  • Make coordinate labels readable as primary content
  • Put bloom colors on text (they're background-only)
  • Use white (#FFFFFF) backgrounds anywhere
  • Mix more than 3 bloom accent colors in one composition
  • Use bloom effects without the underlying dark deep background
  • Add drop shadows to cards (use subtle border + translate instead)
  • Use generic sans-serif fonts (Inter, Roboto, Arial) in place of Work Sans
