Visual Direction: Neon Drift

Dark, atmospheric, cinematic. Imagine stepping into a premium club at twilight—where deep teal-black backgrounds meet neon accents that glow like stage lights through fog. Pastel gradients refract light like a disco ball at 3am. The mood is the moment between the last rays of sunset and the first laser beams. This is sophisticated nightlife, not rave chaos. Premium electronic music culture distilled into light and color.

Typography System

Display — Unbounded (Google Fonts)

Modern Geometric Display

Bold, futuristic, sharp-edged. Used for headline hierarchy, event titles, and stage presence. This is the visual voice of the festival.

Body — DM Sans (Google Fonts)

Clean Contemporary Sans-Serif

The workhorse typeface. Used for body text, descriptions, metadata, and all editorial content. Maintains legibility and contemporary feel across all sizes.

Accent — Caveat (Google Fonts)

Handwritten Intimacy

For personal, human moments. Quotes, scarcity messages, personal testimonials. Used sparingly to add warmth and human connection to the design.

Data — JetBrains Mono (Google Fonts)

Technical, Precise, Structured

For technical details, metadata labels, hex codes, and data presentation. Adds a systematic, organized feeling to information hierarchy.

Design Guidelines

✓ DO

  • Use neon accents sparingly for maximum impact
  • Keep backgrounds dark and atmospheric
  • Apply fog and glow gradients for depth
  • Use grain texture heavily for cinema feel
  • Create pill-shaped buttons with glow hover
  • Layer dark surfaces with subtle borders
  • Use cool-tinted photography grades
  • Animate gradients and breathing glows

✕ DON'T

  • Use light or white backgrounds
  • Use warm earthy tones as primaries
  • Use serif fonts for headlines
  • Overwhelm with neon (it's glimpses of light)
  • Use flat colors without glow or gradient
  • Mix multiple serif typefaces
  • Create high contrast with bright whites
  • Ignore the grain texture overlay
