Visual Direction

Botanical Dusk merges organic botanical elements with golden hour warmth. The identity draws inspiration from Piknik i Parken's botanical collage aesthetic—scattered wildflower illustrations layered over sunset-toned gradients, vintage film grain, and organic compositions—combined with Curfew's golden hour house music identity.

The mood is a summer afternoon fading into a warm evening: daydreamy but grounded. Think warm serif display fonts, collage-style compositions where photography, botanical line art, and bold typography overlap. Nature meets the dancefloor. Every design element celebrates the transition from day to night, from nature to rhythm.

Do's & Don'ts

These guidelines ensure the Botanical Dusk identity remains cohesive, warm, and effective across all applications.

Do ✓

  • Use botanical elements sparingly (max 2–3 per composition) as decorative accents
  • Mix serif headlines (Playfair) with sans-serif body text for hierarchy
  • Apply warm film grain texture overlays for vintage character
  • Create collage-style compositions with overlapping, rotated elements
  • Alternate between light (Parchment) and dark (Deep Earth) sections
  • Use handwritten Caveat font for playful labels and annotations
  • Layer photography with botanical line art and bold typography
  • Apply warm sepia or golden filters to photography

Don't ✗

  • Use neon or overly saturated colors—stay warm and muted
  • Use sharp geometric shapes without organic softening or botanical elements
  • Use pure white (#FFFFFF) or pure black (#000000) backgrounds
  • Overload layouts with botanical elements—restraint creates elegance
  • Use cold blue-toned or desaturated photography without warm filters
  • Mix too many typefaces—stick to the three-typeface system
  • Apply harsh drop shadows—prefer soft, subtle shadows
  • Use corporate or overly modern visual language
